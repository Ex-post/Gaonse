Rajendra Singh

Dehra Village, Munger Growing Wheat & Spices

"I've been farming this land for over 30 years, just like my father and grandfather before me. When I was approached by Gaon Se to grow wheat without chemicals, I was hesitant at first. But they showed me that traditional methods could still work well in today's world."

Rajendra's wheat is stone-ground into our premium atta flour, preserving all the nutrients that would otherwise be lost in commercial processing.

"What I love most is that my children are now taking interest in farming again. They see that there is respect and dignity in this work, especially when people value traditional methods."

Lakshmi Devi

Bariyarpur, Munger Growing Turmeric & Chillies

"In our village, women have always been the keepers of spice knowledge. My mother taught me how to identify the best turmeric rhizomes and how to dry chilies to preserve their color and heat."

Lakshmi leads a women's cooperative that grows and processes spices using traditional methods. Their hand-ground turmeric has a vibrant color and aroma that can't be matched by commercial products.

"Since working with Gaon Se, we've been able to get fair prices for our spices. This has allowed us to invest in our children's education while continuing our traditional farming practices."

Pramod Kumar

Tarapur, Munger Mustard Cultivation

"My family has been growing mustard for generations. We use the same seed varieties that our ancestors used, which produce smaller yields but have a much stronger flavor and higher oil content."

Pramod's mustard is cold-pressed using traditional wooden ghani presses, which preserve the natural pungency and nutritional benefits of the oil.

"There was a time when we considered switching to hybrid seeds and modern farming methods. But working with Gaon Se has shown us that there's still value in our traditional knowledge. Now my son is learning these methods from me, just as I learned from my father."

Khagaria Farmers Collective

Khagaria District, Bihar Mixed Crops

"Our collective was formed by 15 small-scale farmers who were struggling to get fair prices for their produce. Together, we've been able to maintain traditional farming practices while finding markets that value our methods."

The collective grows a variety of crops including coriander, cumin, and black pepper, practicing crop rotation to maintain soil health naturally.

"The partnership with Gaon Se has been transformative for our community. Not only do we get better prices for our crops, but we've also received training on how to improve our traditional methods without compromising their integrity."

Traditional Farming Methods

Natural Pest Control

Our farmers use companion planting, crop rotation, and natural pest deterrents like neem extract instead of chemical pesticides.

Seed Preservation

Farmers save and exchange indigenous seeds, preserving biodiversity and ensuring plants are naturally adapted to local conditions.

Natural Fertilizers

Cow dung, compost, and green manure provide nutrients to the soil, maintaining its health without chemical fertilizers.
